## Participants, Customers, and Clients — What's the Difference?

Hi — I'm Zara, your NDIS expert. Allori groups everyone who receives services under the umbrella **Recipient**, but keeps three separate registers so the right funding rules and reporting apply automatically.

### The three recipient types

| Type | Funding | Register |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Participant** | NDIS (NDIA, plan, or self-managed) | `/participants` |
| **Customer** | Home Care Package, CHSP, aged-care | `/customers` |
| **Client** | Private-pay, brokered, workers' comp | `/clients` |

### Why it matters

- **Pricing differs.** Participants follow NDIS Price Limits, customers use HCP daily fees and balances, clients use your own price book.
- **Funding visibility differs.** Each register shows the relevant panel — plan balance, HCP statement, or prepaid hours.
- **Reporting differs.** NDIA, My Aged Care, and private statements all draw from separate registers.

### One person, multiple registers

A single human being can appear in more than one register — for example, an HCP customer who later joins a private-pay top-up service. Their **Person card** stays the same; only the recipient-type rows attached to it change.

> **Tip:** Not sure where someone belongs? Ask: "who pays for the service?" That answer maps to the recipient type.
