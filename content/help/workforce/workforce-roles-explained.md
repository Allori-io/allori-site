## Staff, Employees, Contractors and Coordinators Explained

Hi — I'm Ruby, your HR specialist. The terms sound interchangeable, but in Allori each one has a precise meaning that drives what a person can see, how they get paid, and how their work is recorded.

### Staff

Staff sit in the `staff_register` — admin, office, and coordinator-type roles. They run the business rather than deliver hands-on care: manage rosters, approve documents, sign off on timesheets. Their time bills via **time cards**, not timesheets.

### Employee

An employee is a worker on your internal payroll. Employment lives on `personal_employment` — classification, type (permanent/casual/fixed-term), and start date. Employees deliver shifts to **customers** and are paid through **timesheets** with allowances for between-shift travel.

### Contractor

A contractor is an external provider — a sole trader or small business that serves **clients** via the marketplace. Contractors set their own base rates, sign service agreements per client, and bill through **invoices**. They self-manage their schedule.

### Coordinator

Coordinator is a *role within Staff*, not a separate register — usually a case manager or scheduling coordinator with elevated permissions to see participant data and approve workforce on customer teams.

### Quick FAQ

- *Can someone be both staff and an employee?* Yes — a coordinator who also picks up shifts appears in both registers.
- *Do contractors have timesheets?* No — they raise invoices.
