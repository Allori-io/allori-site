## Understanding Key Concepts

### The Person Card
Every individual in the system has a **person card** in the person register. This is the single source of truth for all their personal data. When someone is given a role (customer, employee, participant, etc.), they get a domain-specific view on top of their person card — but all data links back to the same person record.

**Key principle:** Data entered at any stage (prospect, applicant, etc.) carries forward when the person is activated into a new role. There is no double-up of data entry.

### Domains
A domain is a role-specific area of the platform. The main domains are:

| Domain | Who it's for |
|--------|--------------|
| **Customer** | Recipients receiving care from employees (HCP, aged care, private) |
| **Client** | Recipients using contractors via the marketplace |
| **Participant** | NDIS participants managed by coordinators |
| **Employee** | Care workers employed by the organisation |
| **Contractor** | Independent care providers on the marketplace |
| **Staff** | Office/admin staff |
| **Coordinator** | NDIS support coordinators (Chinese wall separation) |
| **Care Partner** | Non-employed providers billing via time cards |
| **Prospect** | Potential recipients being onboarded |
| **Applicant** | Potential workers being recruited |

### Unique Code
Every person and supplier has a 6-character alphanumeric unique code. This code:
- Identifies them in the Xero accounting system
- Is used as the filename for their profile photo
- Appears throughout the platform as a quick identifier

### Chinese Wall
NDIS support coordinators operate behind a "Chinese wall" — their case notes, conversations, and participant data are separated from the service delivery side. This is a compliance requirement for NDIS support coordination.

### The Person is Not the Lead
When someone enquires about care, they become a **lead**. The lead is the person making the enquiry (often a family member or representative). The **prospect** is the person who actually needs care. These are usually different people.
