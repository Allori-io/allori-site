## Understanding Recipient Types

There are three types of care recipients in the platform. The type determines who provides their care and how billing works.

### Customer
- **Funded by:** Home Care Package (HCP), aged care, or private
- **Care provided by:** Employees (employed by the organisation)
- **Billing:** Timesheets (auto-generated from scheduled events)
- **Managed by:** Case managers
- **Unique features:** Plan areas (12 structured care plan sections), occupancy tracking

### Client
- **Funded by:** Private / self-funded
- **Care provided by:** Contractors (independent, via the marketplace)
- **Billing:** Invoices (one per service per contractor)
- **Managed by:** Self-managed with support from case managers
- **Unique features:** Jobs page (post to marketplace), service agreements (mutual signing required), contractor approval workflow

### Participant
- **Funded by:** NDIS
- **Care provided by:** Via coordination
- **Billing:** Time cards (coordinator billing only)
- **Managed by:** Coordinators (behind the Chinese wall)
- **Unique features:** Coordination diary (instead of care diary), simplified goals (no job description/tasks)

### Can someone be more than one type?
Yes. A person can be both a customer AND a client. For example, they might receive some services from employed care workers and other services from contractors.
