## Understanding Provider Types

| Type | Employment | Billing | Serves | Key Differences |
|------|-----------|---------|--------|-----------------|
| **Employee** | Employed by organisation | Timesheets | Customers | Can also be Staff. Gets rostered to shifts. |
| **Staff** | Employed by organisation | Salary | Internal | Office/admin role. Always paired with Employee if doing direct care. |
| **Contractor** | Independent | Invoices | Clients | Self-managed via marketplace. Must sign service agreement with each client. |
| **Coordinator** | Employed | Time cards | Participants | NDIS support coordinators. Chinese wall separation. Case notes only visible to other coordinators. |
| **Care Partner** | Not employed | Timesheets + Time cards | Customers | Similar to staff but not on payroll. Paid based on billed time. |

### Important Rules
- **Employee + Staff** are always paired — you must be an employee to be rostered to billable shifts
- **Contractors** cannot also be employees (and vice versa) — this is a Fair Work compliance requirement
- **Care Partners** and **Coordinators** can combine with either employee or contractor roles
- **Staff cannot roster contractors** — contractors must agree to shifts themselves via the marketplace

### How does someone become a provider?
Providers start as **applicants** and go through the HR pipeline. See Activating an Applicant for the full process.
